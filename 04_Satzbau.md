# 04 - Satzbau (German Sentence Structure & Word Order)

## üìö Basic Concept

**Satzbau** refers to the rules for word order in German sentences. Unlike English, German has stricter rules about where words must appear in a sentence, especially the **position of the verb**.

**Key principles:**
- **Verb position** is the most important rule
- Different sentence types have different verb positions
- Time, manner, and place have a specific order
- Word order changes with subordinate clauses

---

## üéØ Why Is Satzbau Important?

Correct word order:
- Makes your German **grammatically correct**
- Ensures your sentences are **understandable**
- Helps you **sound natural** to native speakers
- Is **critical for B1 exam success**

**Wrong word order = confusing or grammatically incorrect German!**

---

## üìç The Three Main Sentence Types

German has three main sentence types, each with different verb positions:

### 1. **Hauptsatz** (Main Clause)
- Verb in **position 2**
- Can stand alone as a complete sentence

### 2. **Nebensatz** (Subordinate Clause)
- Verb at the **end**
- Cannot stand alone

### 3. **Fragesatz** (Question)
- **Yes/No questions:** Verb in position 1
- **W-questions:** Verb in position 2

---

## üîß How to Build German Sentences

### RULE 1: Verb in Position 2 (Hauptsatz)

**The conjugated verb is ALWAYS in the SECOND position** in a main clause.

**Structure:** Position 1 + **VERB** + Position 3, 4, 5...

**Examples:**

| Position 1 | Position 2 (VERB) | Position 3+ |
|------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Ich | lerne | Deutsch. |
| Heute | lerne | ich Deutsch. |
| In Berlin | wohne | ich seit zwei Jahren. |
| Mein Bruder | arbeitet | in M√ºnchen. |
| Gestern | habe | ich einen Film gesehen. |

**Key Point:** Position 1 can be occupied by different elements (subject, time, place), but the verb is ALWAYS in position 2!

---

### RULE 2: Verb at the End (Nebensatz)

In subordinate clauses (introduced by weil, dass, wenn, etc.), the **conjugated verb goes to the END**.

**Structure:** Konnektor + ... + ... + **VERB**

**Examples:**

1. Ich bleibe zu Hause, **weil** ich krank **bin**.
2. Ich wei√ü, **dass** du morgen **kommst**.
3. **Wenn** ich Zeit **habe**, besuche ich dich.

---

### RULE 3: TeKaMoLo (Time, Manner, Place)

When you have multiple pieces of information (time, manner, place), they follow this order:

**Te**mporal (Time) ‚Üí **Ka**modal (Manner/How) ‚Üí **Lo**kal (Place)

**Examples:**

1. Ich gehe **morgen** (time) **mit dem Bus** (manner) **zur Arbeit** (place).
   *I'm going to work tomorrow by bus.*

2. Wir fahren **n√§chste Woche** (time) **mit dem Auto** (manner) **nach Berlin** (place).
   *We're driving to Berlin next week by car.*

3. Sie lernt **jeden Tag** (time) **flei√üig** (manner) **in der Bibliothek** (place).
   *She studies diligently in the library every day.*

**Memory Tip:** **Te-Ka-Mo-Lo** = **Te**mporal, **Ka**modal, **Mo**dal, **Lo**kal

---

### RULE 4: Pronoun Word Order

When you have **two pronouns** (subject and object), the **nominative pronoun comes before the accusative/dative pronoun**.

**Rule:** Nominative ‚Üí Accusative ‚Üí Dativ

**Examples:**

1. Ich gebe **es** **dir**. (Nom: ich, Akk: es, Dat: dir)
   *I give it to you.*

2. Er zeigt **sie** **mir**. (Nom: er, Akk: sie, Dat: mir)
   *He shows it to me.*

**When you have a NOUN and a PRONOUN:**
**Pronoun comes BEFORE noun!**

**Examples:**

1. Ich gebe **dir** das Buch. (pronoun before noun)
   *I give you the book.*

2. Ich gebe **es** meinem Bruder. (pronoun before noun)
   *I give it to my brother.*

BUT:

3. Ich gebe **meinem Bruder** **das Buch**. (both nouns: Dativ before Akkusativ)
   *I give my brother the book.*

---

### RULE 5: Yes/No Questions (Verb Position 1)

For questions that can be answered with "ja" or "nein," the **verb comes first**.

**Structure:** **VERB** + Subject + ...?

**Examples:**

| VERB (Position 1) | Subject | Rest | Translation |
|-------------------|---------|------|-------------|
| **Kommst** | du | heute? | Are you coming today? |
| **Wohnst** | du | in Berlin? | Do you live in Berlin? |
| **Hast** | du | Zeit? | Do you have time? |
| **Ist** | das | dein Auto? | Is that your car? |

---

### RULE 6: W-Questions (Verb Position 2)

Questions starting with a W-word (Wer, Was, Wann, Wo, Warum, Wie, etc.) have the **verb in position 2**.

**Structure:** W-Word + **VERB** + Subject + ...?

**Examples:**

| W-Word | VERB (Position 2) | Subject | Rest | Translation |
|--------|-------------------|---------|------|-------------|
| **Wer** | **ist** | das? | | Who is that? |
| **Was** | **machst** | du? | | What are you doing? |
| **Wann** | **kommst** | du? | | When are you coming? |
| **Wo** | **wohnst** | du? | | Where do you live? |
| **Warum** | **lernst** | du | Deutsch? | Why are you learning German? |
| **Wie** | **hei√üt** | du? | | What's your name? |

## üí° B1-Level Example Sentences

### Simple Sentences (Verb Position 2)

1. **Ich** lerne Deutsch.
   *I learn German.*

2. **Heute** gehe ich ins Kino.
   *Today I'm going to the cinema.*

3. **In M√ºnchen** wohne ich seit zwei Jahren.
   *I've been living in Munich for two years.*

4. **Mein Bruder** arbeitet in einer Bank.
   *My brother works in a bank.*

5. **Am Wochenende** besuche ich meine Eltern.
   *On the weekend, I visit my parents.*

### Complex Sentences with TeKaMoLo

1. Ich gehe **morgen** (time) **mit dem Bus** (manner) **zur Arbeit** (place).
   *I'm going to work tomorrow by bus.*

2. Wir fahren **n√§chsten Monat** (time) **mit dem Auto** (manner) **nach Italien** (place).
   *We're driving to Italy next month by car.*

3. Sie lernt **jeden Abend** (time) **alleine** (manner) **zu Hause** (place).
   *She studies alone at home every evening.*

### Perfekt (Two-Part Verbs)

When using Perfekt (or modal verbs), the **conjugated verb stays in position 2**, and the **participle/infinitive goes to the END**.

**Structure:** Subject + **haben/sein** (pos. 2) + ... + **Partizip** (end)

**Examples:**

1. Ich **habe** gestern einen Film **gesehen**.
   *I saw a movie yesterday.*

2. Wir **sind** nach Berlin **gefahren**.
   *We drove to Berlin.*

3. Sie **hat** viel Deutsch **gelernt**.
   *She learned a lot of German.*

### Modal Verbs

**Structure:** Subject + **Modal Verb** (pos. 2) + ... + **Infinitive** (end)

**Examples:**

1. Ich **muss** heute **arbeiten**.
   *I have to work today.*

2. Du **kannst** gut Deutsch **sprechen**.
   *You can speak German well.*

3. Wir **wollen** morgen ins Kino **gehen**.
   *We want to go to the cinema tomorrow.*

### Questions

**Yes/No Questions:**

1. **Kommst** du morgen?
   *Are you coming tomorrow?*

2. **Hast** du Zeit?
   *Do you have time?*

3. **Wohnst** du in Berlin?
   *Do you live in Berlin?*

**W-Questions:**

1. **Wann kommst** du?
   *When are you coming?*

2. **Wo wohnst** du?
   *Where do you live?*

3. **Warum lernst** du Deutsch?
   *Why are you learning German?*

## üéì Special Cases & Advanced Rules

### 1. Inversion (Verb Before Subject)

When something other than the subject is in position 1, the subject and verb switch places (**inversion**).

**Normal:** Ich gehe heute ins Kino.
**Inversion:** **Heute** gehe **ich** ins Kino.

**The pattern:** Position 1 (not subject) + Verb + Subject + ...

**More Examples:**

1. **Morgen** besuche **ich** meine Eltern.
2. **In Berlin** wohne **ich** seit zwei Jahren.
3. **Gestern** habe **ich** einen Film gesehen.

### 2. Nebensatz First ‚Üí Verb Position 1 in Hauptsatz

When a Nebensatz comes BEFORE the Hauptsatz, the VERB in the Hauptsatz moves to **position 1** (because the entire Nebensatz counts as position 1).

**Structure:** [Nebensatz], **VERB** + Subject + ...

**Examples:**

1. **Weil es regnet**, **bleibe** ich zu Hause.
   *(Because it's raining, I'm staying home.)*

2. **Wenn ich Zeit habe**, **besuche** ich dich.
   *(When I have time, I'll visit you.)*

3. **Obwohl sie m√ºde ist**, **arbeitet** sie weiter.
   *(Although she's tired, she continues working.)*

### 3. Two Objects (Dativ + Akkusativ)

When you have both an indirect object (Dativ) and a direct object (Akkusativ):

**If BOTH are NOUNS:** Dativ ‚Üí Akkusativ
- Ich gebe **meinem Bruder** (Dat) **das Buch** (Akk).

**If one is a PRONOUN:** Pronoun ‚Üí Noun
- Ich gebe **es** (Akk pronoun) **meinem Bruder** (Dat noun).
- Ich gebe **ihm** (Dat pronoun) **das Buch** (Akk noun).

**If BOTH are PRONOUNS:** Akkusativ ‚Üí Dativ
- Ich gebe **es** (Akk) **ihm** (Dat).

### 4. Separable Verbs

Separable verbs split: the **prefix goes to the END**, the verb stays in **position 2**.

**Examples:**

| Infinitive | Sentence | Translation |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| **aufstehen** | Ich **stehe** um 7 Uhr **auf**. | I get up at 7 o'clock. |
| **mitkommen** | **Kommst** du **mit**? | Are you coming along? |
| **anrufen** | Ich **rufe** dich **an**. | I'll call you. |
| **einkaufen** | Wir **kaufen** heute **ein**. | We're shopping today. |

## üìñ Summary: Key Satzbau Rules

| Rule | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Verb Position 2** | Main clause verb in position 2 | **Ich** lerne Deutsch. |
| **Verb at End** | Subordinate clause verb at end | ..., weil ich krank **bin**. |
