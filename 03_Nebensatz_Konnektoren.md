# 03 - Nebensatz & Konnektoren (Subordinate Clauses & Connectors)

## üìö Basic Concept

A **Nebensatz** (subordinate clause) is a dependent clause that cannot stand alone as a complete sentence. It is introduced by a **Konnektor** (connector/conjunction) and always has the **verb at the end**.

**Key features:**
- Cannot stand alone (incomplete thought)
- Introduced by a subordinating conjunction (weil, dass, wenn, etc.)
- **Verb goes to the END** of the clause
- Always separated by a **comma**
- Depends on a Hauptsatz (main clause) to make sense

**vs. Hauptsatz (Main Clause):**
- Can stand alone (complete thought)
- Verb in **position 2** (standard German word order)

---

## üéØ Why Are Nebens√§tze Used?

Nebens√§tze allow you to:
- **Express complex ideas** (cause, reason, time, condition)
- **Connect thoughts** logically
- **Sound more fluent** and achieve B1/B2-level German
- **Answer questions** like Why? When? Although? If?

**Example:**
- Simple: *Ich bleibe zu Hause. Es regnet.*
- Complex: *Ich bleibe zu Hause, **weil** es **regnet**.* (I stay home because it's raining.)

---

## üìç Where Are Nebens√§tze Used?

Nebens√§tze appear:
- In **everyday conversation** to explain reasons, conditions, and timing
- In **written German** (emails, letters, essays)
- In **B1 TELC exam** (especially Schreiben and Sprechen sections)
- Whenever you want to **combine two related ideas**

---

## üîß How to Build a Nebensatz

### Structure

**Option 1:** Hauptsatz + , + Konnektor + ... + **Verb**.

**Example:**
Ich bleibe zu Hause**,** **weil** es **regnet**.
*(I stay home because it's raining.)*

**Option 2:** Konnektor + ... + **Verb** , + **Verb** + ...

**Example:**
**Weil** es **regnet**, **bleibe** ich zu Hause.
*(Because it's raining, I stay home.)*

**CRITICAL RULE:** When the Nebensatz comes FIRST, the verb in the Hauptsatz moves to position 1!

## üéì Special Rules & Tips

### 1. WENN vs. ALS

**WENN** = when/if (repeated actions, present/future, or conditions)
- Repeated: **Wenn** ich in Berlin bin, besuche ich meine Freunde. (every time)
- Future: **Wenn** ich Zeit habe, rufe ich dich an. (if/when)
- Condition: **Wenn** es regnet, bleiben wir zu Hause. (if)

**ALS** = when (ONE TIME in the past only!)
- **Als** ich jung war, lebte ich in M√ºnchen. (one period in the past)
- **Als** ich gestern nach Hause kam, war niemand da. (one specific time)

**Memory Tip:** ALS = "ALmost past" (one time), WENN = "WhenEver" (repeated/future)

### 2. Word Order When Nebensatz Comes First

**CRITICAL RULE:** When the Nebensatz comes BEFORE the Hauptsatz, the verb in the Hauptsatz must be in **position 1**!

‚úÖ **Weil** es regnet, **bleibe** ich zu Hause.
‚ùå Weil es regnet, ich bleibe zu Hause.

**Think of it as:** The entire Nebensatz = Position 1, so the verb comes next!

### 3. Two Verbs in Nebensatz (Modal Verbs, Perfekt)

When there are **two verbs** (modal verb + infinitive, or Perfekt), they BOTH go to the end:

**Modal Verb:**
Ich lerne Deutsch, weil ich in Deutschland **arbeiten kann**.
*(I learn German because I can work in Germany.)*

**Perfekt:**
Er ist m√ºde, weil er viel **gearbeitet hat**.
*(He is tired because he has worked a lot.)*

**Order:** Participle/Infinitive + Helping Verb (haben/sein/modal)

### 4. Commas with Nebensatz

Always use a comma to separate Hauptsatz and Nebensatz:

‚úÖ Ich bleibe zu Hause**,** weil es regnet.
‚úÖ Weil es regnet**,** bleibe ich zu Hause.

### 5. DASS vs. DAS

**DASS** = that (conjunction - introduces Nebensatz)
- Ich wei√ü, **dass** du Recht hast.

**DAS** = the/that (article or demonstrative pronoun)
- **Das** Buch ist interessant.
- Ich mag **das**.

**Memory Tip:** If you can replace it with "welches," use DAS (article). Otherwise, DASS (conjunction).
