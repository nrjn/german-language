# 00 - Nomen-Logik: German Noun Gender Rules

## üìö Basic Concept

Every German noun has a **gender**: masculine (der), feminine (die), or neuter (das). Unlike English, the gender is **grammatical**, not biological (a girl = das M√§dchen, neuter!).

**Why is this important?**
- The gender determines which articles, adjectives, and pronouns you use
- It's essential for correct case declension
- It's one of the most challenging aspects of German for learners!

**The good news:** About **70-80% of German nouns follow predictable patterns!**

## üéì Special Cases & Tricks

| Compound | Gender | Why? |
|----------|--------|------|

| **der** Sonnenaufgang | masculine | der Aufgang (rise) is masculine |
