# 05 - Verben Perfekt (Perfect Tense)

## ğŸ“š Basic Concept

The **Perfekt** (present perfect) is the most common past tense used in **spoken German** and informal writing. It describes completed actions in the past.

**Formation:** **haben/sein** (conjugated) + ... + **Partizip II** (past participle at the end)

**Examples:**
- Ich **habe** einen Film **gesehen**. (I saw a movie / I have seen a movie.)
- Wir **sind** nach Berlin **gefahren**. (We drove to Berlin / We have driven to Berlin.)

---

## ğŸ¯ Why Is Perfekt Used?

Perfekt is used to:
- Talk about **past actions** in everyday conversation
- Describe **completed events**
- Express **past experiences**
- Tell stories in informal settings

**In conversation, Germans prefer Perfekt over PrÃ¤teritum** (simple past)!

**PrÃ¤teritum** is mainly used for:
- Formal writing (newspapers, books, reports)
- The verbs "sein," "haben," and modal verbs (even in speech)

## ğŸ”§ How to Form Perfekt

### Step 1: Choose haben or sein

**MOST verbs use HABEN (about 99%):**
- Ich **habe** gekauft, gelernt, gesehen, gegessen, etc.

**ONLY these verbs use SEIN (about 1%):**

1. **Verbs of motion** (movement from A to B):
   - gehen, fahren, laufen, fliegen, kommen, reisen, etc.

2. **Verbs of change of state** (becoming something different):
   - werden, sterben, wachsen, aufwachen, einschlafen, etc.

3. **Exceptions** (memorize these!):
   - **sein** (to be) â†’ Ich bin gewesen.
   - **bleiben** (to stay) â†’ Ich bin geblieben.
   - **passieren** (to happen) â†’ Es ist passiert.

**IMPORTANT:** sein verbs describe a change in LOCATION or STATE!

### Step 2: Form the Partizip II (Past Participle)

**Pattern:** ge- + stem + -(e)t

### Step 3: Word Order in Perfekt

**Main Clause:** haben/sein (position 2) + ... + Partizip II (end)

**Examples:**
- Ich **habe** gestern einen Film **gesehen**.
- Wir **sind** nach Berlin **gefahren**.
- Heute **haben** wir viel **gelernt**.

**Subordinate Clause:** ... + Partizip II + haben/sein (end)

**Examples:**

## ğŸ’¡ B1-Level Example Sentences

### HABEN Verbs (Most Common)

1. Ich **habe** Deutsch **gelernt**.
   *I learned German / I have learned German.*

2. Sie **hat** ein Buch **gelesen**.
   *She read a book / She has read a book.*

3. Wir **haben** Pizza **gegessen**.
   *We ate pizza / We have eaten pizza.*

4. Er **hat** einen Brief **geschrieben**.
   *He wrote a letter / He has written a letter.*

5. Ihr **habt** viel **gearbeitet**.
   *You (plural) worked a lot / You have worked a lot.*

6. Ich **habe** meine Oma **besucht**.
   *I visited my grandma / I have visited my grandma.*

7. Sie **haben** Medizin **studiert**.
   *They studied medicine / They have studied medicine.*

### SEIN Verbs (Motion & Change of State)

1. Ich **bin** nach Berlin **gefahren**.
   *I drove to Berlin / I have driven to Berlin.*

2. Wir **sind** ins Kino **gegangen**.
   *We went to the cinema / We have gone to the cinema.*

3. Er **ist** Lehrer **geworden**.
   *He became a teacher / He has become a teacher.*

4. Sie **ist** um 7 Uhr **aufgestanden**.
   *She got up at 7 o'clock / She has gotten up at 7 o'clock.*

5. Das Auto **ist** schnell **gefahren**.
   *The car drove fast / The car has driven fast.*

6. Wir **sind** zu Hause **geblieben**.
   *We stayed at home / We have stayed at home.*

7. Es **ist** gestern **passiert**.
   *It happened yesterday / It has happened yesterday.*

### Mixed Examples (Conversation)

1. **Was hast du gestern gemacht?**
   - Ich **bin** ins Kino **gegangen** und **habe** einen Film **gesehen**.
   *What did you do yesterday?*
   *I went to the cinema and saw a movie.*

2. **Wo hast du gewohnt?**
   - Ich **habe** zwei Jahre in MÃ¼nchen **gewohnt**.
   *Where did you live?*
   *I lived in Munich for two years.*

3. **Bist du schon in Berlin gewesen?**
   - Ja, ich **bin** letztes Jahr dort **gewesen**.
   *Have you been to Berlin before?*
   *Yes, I was there last year.*
